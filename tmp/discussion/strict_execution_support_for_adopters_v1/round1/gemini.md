Verdict: The framework has an exceptionally strong, pipeline-enforced governance baseline (Layer A) for state-sync and closeout truth, but its execution layer relies too heavily on agent goodwill and policy adherence (Layers B and C). Downstream agents currently have too much room to blame the template for execution ambiguity because the framework dictates rules (e.g., "use verified toolchains", "record leftovers", "seek independent evaluation") without providing the mechanical wrappers and stop-gates required to enforce them autonomously.

| Priority | Proposal | Solves | Why current support is insufficient | Suggested surface | Adoption tier | Governance risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Recommended next move:
Implement the **Toolchain Verifier** and **Skill Execution Wrapper** first. These two surfaces directly eliminate the most common downstream execution excuses—"I didn't have the right command" or "I forgot to log the outcome"—by forcing the target repo to prove its toolchain works and forcing the agent to execute through a receipt-generating boundary. To avoid dangerously bypassing governance, ensure the execution wrapper *only* generates invocation evidence and never autonomously authorizes canonical SKILL mutation. By mechanizing these exact failure points, the template stops relying on agent goodwill and shifts the burden of proof to auditable, pipeline-enforced artifacts, effectively bridging the gap between design intent and honest execution.
